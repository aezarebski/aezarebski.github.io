Very rough notes about variants

B.1.617.1 variant

  • Kappa variant
  • Responsible for Melbourne outbreak in May 2021.

B.1.617 variant

  • First identified in Maharashtra, India in October 2020.
  • Has mutation E484Q and L452R.

B.1.351 (501.V2) variant

Also known as The South African Variant and B.1.351) has important mutations: N501Y, K417N, and E484K. The Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccine appears to have reduced efficacy against this variant.

B.1.1.7 variant

  • doi: https://doi.org/10.1101/2021.06.06.446826 suggests mutations outside of the spike protein might be supressing the innate immune response.
  • Also known as the Kent variant due to the location it was first detected in.
  • Also, also known as 501Y.V1 because it has the N501Y mutation.
  • B.1.1.7 has the E484K E484K mutation.

E484K

  • This refers to a mutation which appears to assist the virus with escaping antibodies developed in response to previous forms.
  • The B.1.1.7 variant and the B.1.351 variant have this mutation.

E484Q

  • See E484K, this is the same location but a different aminoacid being used.
  • The B.1.617 variant has this mutation.
