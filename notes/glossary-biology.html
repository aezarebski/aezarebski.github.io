Biology and Epidemiology Glossary

A

Allele

  • An allele is a variant of a gene.

B

C

Clonal species

  • Clonal species do not exchange DNA across lineages; rather, diversity is driven solely by mutational processes.1.

D

Diploid

  • See Ploidy.

E

EBOV

  • An abbreviation for Ebola virus, not to be confused with EVD.

EVD

  • An acronym for Ebola virus disease, not to be confused with EBOV.

Extinction rate

  • The rate at which live species go extinct.
  • Equivalent to the death rate in a birth-death process.

F

G

Gene

Generation interval

  • The time between the infection time of an infector and the infection time of the person they infect. Not to be confused with the serial interval.

H

Haploid

  • See Ploidy.

Haplotype

  • A collection of alleles inherited from a single parent.

Horizontal gene transfer (HGT)

I

J

K

L

Lie-to-children

  • A simplified explanation of a technical or complex subject used to provide a foundation for deeper understanding. For example, saying "the Earth is a sphere", while not true is useful.

M

N

O

P

Ploidy

  • The number of complete sets of chromosomes in a cell, for example, monoploid organisms have one complete set, diploid have two and polyploid have three or more. Note that monoploid refers to having one complete set while haploid refers to a gamete with half the normal set of chromosomes.

Polymorphism

  • The appearance of multiple different phenotypes in a population2.

Polytomy

  • An internal node of a phylogeny with more than two descendants.
  • An example of a hard polytomy is found in some species of the Drosophilia genus: the simulans species complex.

Q

R

Rinderpest

  • Also known as cattle-plague, this is a virus with a very high mortality that infects cattle and buffalo. It was the second virus to be eradicated by the human species.3

S

Serial interval

  • The time between the onset of symptoms of someone and the person they infected. Not to be confused with the generation interval.

Single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP

Speciation rate

  • The rate at which new species form.
  • Equivalent to the birth rate in a birth-death process.

Spike protein

  • A glycoprotein found on the surface of a virus (notably SARS-CoV-2, but also EBV, and Influenza virus) which plays an important role in the virus entering the cell4.

Synapomorphy

  • A novel genotype that has been inherited by at least two daughter taxa. It is novel in the sense that it should not have occurred more than once.

W

Whole genome sequencing (WGS)

  • Whole genome sequencing (WGS), also known as full genome sequencing, complete genome sequencing, or entire genome sequencing, is the process of determining the entirety, or nearly the entirety, of the DNA sequence of an organism's genome at a single time.5
